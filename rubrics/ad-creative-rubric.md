# Ad Creative Rubric

For paid social frames, static ads, and video stills (Meta, TikTok, YouTube, display).

An ad lives or dies in the first half-second of a scroll. The hook and immediate clarity carry the most weight; polish is necessary but not sufficient.

## Weighted categories

| Category | Weight | What it measures |
|----------|:------:|------------------|
| Hook strength | 20% | Does it stop the scroll? |
| Product clarity | 15% | Is it obvious what's being sold? |
| Brand fit | 15% | Does it match the brand's voice and look? |
| Visual composition | 15% | Is the frame composed, focused, and intentional? |
| Emotional pull | 10% | Does it create tension, desire, or curiosity? |
| Originality | 10% | Is it distinctive, or a generic ad template? |
| Platform fit | 10% | Is it built for where it will run? |
| Production quality | 5% | Is the craft clean enough to look credible? |

Weights sum to 100%. Category scores are 0–10; the weighted total maps to the 0–100 taste score.

## Evaluate

- **Scroll-stopping power.** In a fast feed, does anything arrest the eye in the first frame — motion, contrast, face, tension, pattern interrupt? A polished ad that blends in fails the hook.
- **First-read clarity.** Within one second, is the subject legible? Flag frames that require study to decode.
- **Product visibility.** Is the actual product hero, recognizable, and shown at a flattering, truthful angle? Flag ads where the product is tiny, cropped, or buried under effects.
- **Offer clarity.** If there's an offer or value prop, is it instantly readable at thumbnail size? Flag offers lost in small or low-contrast type.
- **Emotional tension.** Does it provoke a feeling — aspiration, problem-recognition, FOMO, delight? Flat, purely descriptive ads underperform.
- **Platform appropriateness.** Right aspect ratio, safe zones for UI overlays, native vs over-produced tone for the platform. Flag a TV-spot frame dropped into a TikTok feed.
- **Generic feel.** Flag the template tells: floating product on gradient, three-emoji headline, default bold-sans-on-photo, AI-stock model with uncanny hands.
- **Would it work as a paid ad.** The integrating question: would you spend real budget putting this in front of cold traffic and expect a result? If not, it's a `revise` at best.

## Reviewer notes

- A strong hook with mediocre polish often beats a polished ad with no hook. Weight accordingly.
- Score the *frame* on its own merits, but note where motion or sequence would change the verdict if this is a video still.
