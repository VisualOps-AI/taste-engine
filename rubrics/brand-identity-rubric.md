# Brand Identity Rubric

For logos, marks, wordmarks, and identity systems.

Identity work is judged on whether it is distinctive, memorable, strategically right, and built to scale across every surface the brand will touch — for years, not for one mockup.

## Weighted categories

| Category | Weight | What it measures |
|----------|:------:|------------------|
| Distinctiveness | 20% | Does it stand apart in its category? |
| Memorability | 15% | Can someone recall and redraw it? |
| Strategic fit | 15% | Does it express the brand's positioning and audience? |
| Typography | 15% | Is the type custom, considered, and ownable? |
| Scalability | 10% | Does it hold up large, small, and in one color? |
| Color system | 10% | Is the palette intentional, flexible, and ownable? |
| Visual consistency | 10% | Does the system cohere across applications? |
| Practical usability | 5% | Is it easy to apply in the real world? |

Weights sum to 100%. Category scores are 0–10; the weighted total maps to the 0–100 taste score.

## Evaluate

- **Logo strength.** Is the core mark a clear, confident idea — or a generic icon plus a default font? Flag clip-art metaphors and stock symbol + Helvetica combinations.
- **Brand system coherence.** Do logo, type, color, and supporting elements feel like one designed system? Flag mismatched parts that don't belong to the same brand.
- **Premium vs cheap feel.** Does the identity read as considered and high-quality, or templated and disposable? Name the specific signals (spacing, weight, restraint, finish).
- **Category differentiation.** Place it next to direct competitors. Does it own a distinct space, or blend into category conventions (every fintech blue, every wellness sans-serif lowercase)?
- **Usage across digital and physical.** App icon, favicon, embroidery, signage, packaging, social avatar — does it survive all of them? Flag identities that only work in one hero context.
- **Whether the identity can scale.** Does it work at 16px and on a billboard, in full color and in one ink, animated and static? Flag fragile marks that depend on gradients or fine detail to read.

## Reviewer notes

- Distinctiveness and memorability are the two hardest things to fix later; weight them honestly and don't reward "clean" when it really means "anonymous."
- A technically clean but undifferentiated identity is a `revise`. A distinctive mark with fixable execution issues is also a `revise` — but for the opposite reason. Say which.
