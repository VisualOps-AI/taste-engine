# Landing Page Rubric

For marketing pages, portfolio sites, product pages, and conversion-focused web layouts.

A landing page is judged on whether it communicates and converts, not just whether it looks polished. Clarity, hierarchy, and conversion strength dominate the weighting.

## Weighted categories

| Category | Weight | What it measures |
|----------|:------:|------------------|
| Clarity | 20% | Is it instantly obvious what this is and who it's for? |
| Visual hierarchy | 20% | Does the eye move through the page in the intended order? |
| Conversion strength | 20% | Does the page drive toward a clear, compelling action? |
| Brand trust | 15% | Does it look credible and safe to act on? |
| Typography | 10% | Is type legible, hierarchical, and on-brand? |
| Composition | 10% | Is layout, spacing, and rhythm considered? |
| Color logic | 5% | Is the palette coherent and accessible? |

Weights sum to 100%. Category scores are 0–10; the weighted total maps to the 0–100 taste score.

## Evaluate

- **Above-the-fold clarity.** Within five seconds, can a first-time visitor state what this is, who it's for, and what to do? If not, clarity fails regardless of polish.
- **CTA strength.** Is there one primary action? Is it visually dominant, specifically worded, and repeated at the right moments? Flag competing or vague CTAs ("Submit", "Learn more" as the primary).
- **Trust signals.** Logos, proof, testimonials, security cues, real numbers — present and credible, or absent? Flag empty social proof and placeholder credibility.
- **Scannability.** Can the page be understood by skimming headings alone? Walls of text and undifferentiated sections fail.
- **Information flow.** Does the narrative build logically (problem → solution → proof → action)? Flag disordered or repetitive sections.
- **Visual polish.** Spacing rhythm, alignment, consistent components, intentional whitespace. Flag cramped or arbitrary spacing.
- **Mobile risk.** Will the hierarchy, type scale, and CTA survive a narrow viewport? Flag layouts that obviously break or bury the CTA on mobile.
- **Generic SaaS look.** Flag the default template signals: purple-to-blue gradient hero, three-icon feature row, vague abstract 3D blob, stock "team collaborating" photo, undifferentiated card grid. Generic ≠ trustworthy.
- **Message–market fit.** Does the copy and visual tone match how the target audience actually thinks and buys? Flag mismatch between sophistication of audience and tone of page.

## Reviewer notes

- A beautiful page that doesn't convert is a `revise`. A clear, converting page that's visually plain may still `pass`.
- Distinguish "boring but effective" from "generic and forgettable." The first can ship; the second usually can't compete.
